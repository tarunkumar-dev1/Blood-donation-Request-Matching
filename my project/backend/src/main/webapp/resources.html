ü©∏ Donor Guidelines

Blood donation is a noble act that saves lives. Here's everything you need to know before donating blood.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age between 18-65 years
  • Weight must be at least 50 kg (110 lbs)
  • Hemoglobin level should be 12.5 g/dL or higher
  • Must be in good health and feeling well
  • Blood pressure should be normal (120/80 mmHg)

Before Donation

  • Get adequate sleep (at least 7-8 hours)
  • Eat a healthy meal before donation
  • Drink plenty of water (16 oz before donating)
  • Avoid fatty foods 24 hours before
  • Bring a valid ID proof

After Donation

  • Rest for 10-15 minutes after donation
  • Drink extra fluids for 24-48 hours
  • Avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours
  • Eat iron-rich foods to replenish blood
  • Keep the bandage on for 4-5 hours

Donation Frequency

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks)

Time Required

The entire process takes about 45-60 minutes

Amount Donated

Approximately 450-500 ml (one pint) per donation

üî¥ Blood Group Information

Understanding blood types is crucial for safe blood transfusions and donations.

Blood Type Compatibility

A+ Blood Type

Can donate to: A+, AB+
Can receive from: A+, A-, O+, O-

A- Blood Type

Can donate to: A+, A-, AB+, AB-
Can receive from: A-, O-

B+ Blood Type

Can donate to: B+, AB+
Can receive from: B+, B-, O+, O-

B- Blood Type

Can donate to: B+, B-, AB+, AB-
Can receive from: B-, O-

AB+ Blood Type

Can donate to: AB+
Can receive from: All blood types (Universal Receiver)

AB- Blood Type

Can donate to: AB+, AB-
Can receive from: AB-, A-, B-, O-

O+ Blood Type

Can donate to: O+, A+, B+, AB+
Can receive from: O+, O-

O- Blood Type

Can donate to: All blood types (Universal Donor)
Can receive from: O-

Rh Factor

The Rh factor is a protein found on red blood cells. If you have the protein, you're Rh positive (+). If you don't have it, you're Rh negative (-). About 85% of people are Rh positive.
